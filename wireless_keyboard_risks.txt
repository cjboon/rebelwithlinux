Question: Are wireless keyboards with USB dongles secure, or do they present security risks?

Answer:

Yes, they can be a security issue, especially older ones.

Risks:
- 2.4GHz keyboards (non-Bluetooth): Often unencrypted, vulnerable to keystroke interception
- MouseJack vulnerability: Can inject keystrokes into vulnerable receivers
- Keyloggers: Attackers can capture typed characters from nearby

Risk level by type:
- Old 2.4GHz dongle keyboards: High risk
- Modern 2.4GHz (Logitech etc.): Low-medium risk (encrypted)
- Bluetooth 4.0+: Low risk (encrypted, authenticated)

Recommendations:
- For high security: Use wired keyboard
- If wireless needed: Use Bluetooth 4.0+ or modern encrypted dongle keyboards
- Keep receiver firmware updated
- Don't use for sensitive input on untrusted networks

Practical risk: For most users, the risk is low - requires nearby attacker with specific equipment. But if you're concerned about targeted attacks or handling sensitive data, wired is the safer choice.
