Question: Is it safe to leave a laptop unattended at home, or does it pose security risks?

Answer:

Yes, but it depends on your setup:

Risks if someone gains physical access:
- Extract hard drive and read data
- Bypass login (live USB boot)
- Install hardware keyloggers
- Clone the drive for offline attack

Mitigations:
- Full disk encryption (LUKS/BitLocker) - critical
- Power off, not just sleep/hibernate
- Strong BIOS/boot password
- Keep laptop in a secure location (locked room/safe)

If encrypted and powered off: Low risk. Attacker would need the encryption password to access data.

If not encrypted: High risk. Physical access = data access.

Bottom line: With full-disk encryption and powered off, leaving it at home is relatively safe. Just don't leave it logged in or unencrypted.
