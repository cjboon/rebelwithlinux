Question: Which is more trustworthy for protecting user data - an ISP or a privacy-focused VPN service like Mullvad?

Answer:

Mullvad, by a significant margin.

ISP sees:
- All unencrypted traffic
- DNS queries (every domain you visit)
- Metadata (when, how much, how long)
- Can sell data to advertisers
- Subject to government surveillance requests
- Knows your real identity/address

Mullvad sees:
- Encrypted traffic (can't read it)
- Connects to their server (not your real IP)
- No logs policy (they claim)
- No connection to your identity (anonymous account, no email)
- Swedish jurisdiction (strong privacy laws)

Caveats:
- You're shifting trust from ISP to VPN
- VPN can still see your traffic if they wanted to
- Choose a reputable VPN (Mullvad is good - audited, no-log, accepts cash)
- Government could still compel VPN to log

Even better:
- Use a privacy-focused DNS over HTTPS
- Use Tor for sensitive browsing
- Combine VPN + privacy DNS

Bottom line: ISP has your identity + all traffic metadata. VPN has neither (if reputable). Trust the VPN more than ISP for privacy.
