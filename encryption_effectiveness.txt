Question: How effective is modern encryption at protecting data?

Answer:

Modern encryption is very strong when properly implemented.

Symmetric (AES-256):
- Considered unbreakable with current technology
- Used for full-disk encryption, file encryption
- NSA-approved for classified info

Asymmetric (RSA-4096, Curve25519):
- Secure for key exchange, signatures
- Quantum computers will eventually threaten RSA
- Post-quantum algorithms emerging

Common implementations:
- LUKS (Linux disk encryption) - very strong
- BitLocker - strong (closed source, but audited)
- GPG - strong for files/email
- Signal - strong for messaging

Weak points (not the math):
- Weak passwords/phrases
- Keyloggers, social engineering
- Implementation bugs
- Side-channel attacks
- Metadata/headers leak info

Bottom line: The math is solid. The risk is usually password strength, key management, or implementation flaws - not the encryption itself being broken.

For most users: AES-256 with a strong passphrase is more than sufficient.
